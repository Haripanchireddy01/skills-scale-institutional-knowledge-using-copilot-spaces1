# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers ensure products meet user needs through research, prototyping, and design. They bridge the gap between business requirements, end-users, and technical teams to create intuitive experiences.

### Responsibilities
- Drive user research, prototyping, usability testing, and design hand-off
- Bridge the gap between business requirements, end-users, and technical teams
- Create wireframes, mockups, and interactive prototypes
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Maintain design systems and ensure consistency

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, user-centered designs
- Reduce user friction and improve satisfaction
- Ensure design consistency across the product

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and critique sessions
- Collaboration with Product Managers on requirements
- Design hand-off sessions with Developers
- Usability testing sessions with users

### Interactions
- Collaborates closely with Product Managers for requirements and user stories
- Works with Developers for implementation feasibility and design hand-off
- Partners with QA for usability testing and acceptance criteria validation

---

## DevOps Engineer

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers build and maintain infrastructure, automation, and deployment systems. They enable fast, safe, and reliable software delivery while ensuring system reliability and security.

### Responsibilities
- Build and maintain CI/CD pipelines and automation
- Manage infrastructure as code and cloud resources
- Ensure system reliability, scalability, and performance
- Monitor production health and respond to incidents
- Implement security best practices in deployment processes

### Goals
- Enable fast, safe, and reliable deployments
- Minimize downtime and ensure high availability
- Improve developer productivity through automation

### Typical Communication
- Deployment planning and release coordination
- Incident response and post-mortems
- Infrastructure and tooling discussions
- Security and compliance reviews

### Interactions
- Works closely with Developers for deployment automation and monitoring
- Partners with QA for test automation infrastructure
- Collaborates with Project Managers for planning release cycles and deployment windows
- Coordinates with Security teams on compliance

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts analyze business processes and translate requirements into actionable solutions. They bridge communication between stakeholders and engineering teams to ensure technical solutions meet business objectives.

### Responsibilities
- Analyze business processes and identify improvement opportunities
- Document detailed requirements and user stories
- Support solution definition between stakeholders and engineering
- Translate business needs into actionable technical tasks
- Validate solutions against business requirements

### Goals
- Ensure technical solutions meet business objectives
- Bridge communication gaps between business and technical teams
- Improve process efficiency and effectiveness

### Typical Communication
- Requirements gathering sessions with stakeholders
- Documentation and specification reviews
- Backlog refinement and user story workshops
- Business case presentations

### Interactions
- Partners with Product Managers for backlog refinement and prioritization
- Works with Developers for requirement clarification and feasibility assessment
- Collaborates with Stakeholders for validation and approval
- Supports Project Managers with scope definition

---

## How these personas are used in the exercise
- Use these persona definitions to frame scenarios and sample interactions in the Skills Exercise.
- Each persona can be used as a persona prompt for Copilot Spaces to shape role-specific guidance.
- The six personas (Developers, Product Managers, Project Managers, UX Designers, DevOps Engineers, and Business Analysts) provide comprehensive coverage of cross-functional collaboration in modern software delivery.
